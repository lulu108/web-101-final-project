Drones are Male

The second type of bee is male bees known as drones. They are unfertilized eggs and live only a few months out of the year. They are recognizable due to their strange and large eyes. The drone's only duty is to spread its genes in the colony and find a new queen to mate with. Their eyes cover their entire face, which helps them to spot queens easily while airborne. Another difference between female workers and drones is the latter don't have stingers (Jernigan).
